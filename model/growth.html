Bacterial Growth Analytic Model

Introduction

   The purpose of building a bacterial growth analytic model is to simulate and analyze the experiment result. We use Simbiology toolbox in Matlab to simulate the genetic regulation and fit the experiment data into our model. At the end of the model, we can get the mutation frequency from our experiment, and we compare it to the score of machine learning result.

The steps of building bacterial growth analytic model

   The following flow chart shows the steps of building our bacterial growth analytic model. First of all, we use IPTG to induce toxin gene. However, the addition of IPTG will inhibit the growth of E. coli. Hence, we do a new experiment without the toxin gene to deal with this problem. Second, we calculate the effect of the toxin gene to get the natural mutation frequency of the bacteria. Third, we also take the inhibition effect of the chemical into consideration as a constant. In the end, we combine the parameters we have got to get the mutation parameter caused by chemicals.

The Effect of IPTG

Growth Curve Without Biobrick and IPTG Induction

   This model is mainly focusing on the effect of IPTG because IPTG will inhibit the bacterial growth curve. Thus, we compare the E. coli with biobrick and without IPTG induction to get the parameter of IPTG’s toxicity. The equation below is the formula we use in Matlab to fit the data from our experiment.

$$\frac{dB}{dt} = g\cdot B(1-\frac{B}{B_{Max}})$$

   The model is about the growth rate of microbe in an isolated environment. As we can see above, the growth rate "g" increases as time goes by when the population of E. coli is significantly smaller than BMax, which is the maximum capacity of the isolated environment. However, the growth rate "g" decreases when the amount of E. coli approaches to BMax. That causes the higher the population is, the more remarkable the effect of nutrient limitation is.

IPTG Inhibits Bacterial Growth

   Getting the parameter g and BMax of pSB1A3 from the previous part, we can use the parameter to estimate the toxicity of IPTG in the following part further.

$$\frac{dB}{dt} = g\cdot B(1-\frac{B}{B_{Max}})-T_{IPTG}\cdot[IPTG]\cdot B$$

   In this step, we assume that part of the bacteria will die due to the toxicity of IPTG. We are using the Mass Action Law to descript the inhibition effect. The differential equation is shown above, where TIPTG is the rate constant of IPTG inhibition, and [IPTG]．B is the Reaction Quotient.

Bacterial Growth Parameters with Different Inserted Gene

   The E. coli colonies with different inserted genes may have different bacterial growth curve, so we do the experiment to observe the growth curve with different inserted gene.

Figure 4: Comparison of different inserted gene

   As we can see above, we compare the bacterial growth curve, and we found that the statistical significance “p-value” of each curve and the average are all above 0.05, so we can prove that bacteria with different inserted toxin gene don’t have statistical significance, which means the experiment sample can be regarded as the same, so that we can use the general growth curve to calculate our bacterial growth curve.

The Effect of Chemical

   As a result of the effect of the chemicals on our growth curve, not only by offering a mutation rate but also by killing some bacteria in the process, we firstly compare the growth curve of the bacteria with and without chemical to get the parameter of chemical inhibition (Tchem). Then, we fit the growth curve with a mutation rate (M2), which caused by chemicals.

   We assume that the chemical inhibition effect is constant, and we fit our experiment data with the formula below.

$$\frac{dB}{dt}=g\cdot B(1-\frac{B}{B_{Max}})-T_{chem}\cdot B$$
