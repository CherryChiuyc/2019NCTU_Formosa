Characterization

Introduction

   To achieve the standard of characterization. This year, we measured the function of BioBrick BBa_I6060, a kind of yellow fluorescent protein. In our plan, first, we resuspended the DNA from the distribution kit and then transformed it into E. coli DH5α. Due to the lack of LacI gene inside E. coli DH5α, the Plac promoter can be view as a constitutive promoter that can thus express downstream genes continuously. After transforming, we ran colony PCR and SDS PAGE to confirm that we successfully produced our target protein. Finally, we used the ELISA reader - CLARIOstar® to detect different expression parameters of yellow fluorescent protein through time.

Experiment design

   In our experiment design, we separate it into two parts: the first is colony PCR and SDS Page, which is used to see if we produce our target or not. The second is optical density(OD) and fluorescence, and they are used to confirm the function of the yellow fluorescence protein.

A. Colony PCR and SDS PAGE

   In this part, we confirmed the nucleotide sequence size and protein size by using colony PCR and SDS PAGE. First, we get the biobrick BBa_I6060 from the distribution kit and then transform it to E. coli DH5α. Then we ran both experiments simultaneously. The colony PCR and SDS PAGE protocol can be seen on our protocol page on our wiki.

B. Optical density and fluorescence

   Confirming the size of the gene and the protein size are not enough for us to make sure the protein works fine in E. coli. Therefore, we measure the OD value and fluorescence to make sure the protein’s functionality and the amount of the fluorescent protein produced over periods. In the beginning, we incubate our yellow fluorescent protein E. coli and pSB1C3 backbone for 12 hours. Then, we dilute the sample to six tubes with LB broth to 6 mL in a ratio of 1:100. After dilution, we start to measure the optical density and fluorescence. For each time, we take a 200μL sample and place it into a 96-well plate (costar, flat bottom with lid). Finally, we put the 96-well plate into the CLARIOstar® Plus plate reader to measure OD and fluorescence. The parameter for fluorescence is shown below:

Figure 4: results of the fluorescence intensity of eYFP over time

Figure 5: relationship between eYFP and OD 600

   According to the results, in figure 4, the fluorescence of eYFP has been increased in a constant speed, and the trend is similar to the OD value, we guess it’s because it has a constitutive promoter, so the amount of protein will be highly related to the amount of the bacteria. Not only the fluorescence intensity keeps increasing while the OD is increasing, but it also reaches the maximum value at the same time as the OD value.

   In figure 5, the relation between optical density and fluorescence intensity is 0.692, and it belongs to moderate correlated. Although it’s not entirely agreed with what we suspect, it’s still close to highly correlated, so we think it’s just because of the error while we’re measuring.

Conclusion

   After all these measurements, we proved that the biobrick in the kit could function normally inside E. coli DH5α, and produce apparent yellow fluorescence. We hope our experimentation will help to improve the accessibility and comparability of reliable data in synthetic biology labs across the world.
