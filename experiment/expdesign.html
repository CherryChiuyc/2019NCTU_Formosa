Experiment Design

BioBrick

   In Quorum Sensing (QS) system part, LuxI protein and LuxR protein will be produced continuously as E. coli grows under the constitutive promoter. LuxI protein catalysts the process of turning S-Adenosyl methionine (SAM) into Acyl Homoserine Lactones (AHL). As long as the amount of the E. coli reaches the threshold, there will be enough AHL to combine with LuxR protein and form LuxR-AHL complex, which can induce the Lux promoter and thus enables RFP to be produced. That is, E. coli will display red fluorescence provided that its culture is large enough.

   Toxin gene is promoted by lac promoter and IPTG will be added to induce toxin gene when the growth of bacteria reaches the desired stage (O.D.=0.3) during our experiment, making the population of E. coli raise slowly or even stop raising. However, some mutation might happen spontaneously after a period of time, causing the malfunction or deactivation of toxin gene. As a result, the amount of the bacteria would increase again. The higher the mutation potential is, the more likely the toxin genes will be mutated and thus losses function, making the population of E. coli increase faster. Combining QS system and toxin gene, we are able to know whether mutation has happened by detecting red fluorescence.

   To sum up, the higher the mutation potential is, the faster the population of E. coli will reach the threshold and thus begin to express red fluorescence.

Quorum Sensing

   Quorum sensing (QS) is a gene expression system responding to the fluctuation of cell density. This unique system enables bacteria to communicate with each other, but in a different way. Unlike we human communicate through talking, bacteria connect each other by coordinating gene expression. The existence of certain chemical signal such as Acylated Homoserine Lactone (AHL) will indirectly affect whether to express certain genes and thus serves as a way to represent the cell density.

Toxin-antitoxin System

   In our project, six kinds of toxin genes are applied, including Mazf, ccdB, ydfD, yafQ, chpBK and hipA. Except ydfD, all toxic genes used are members of Toxin-antitoxin system (TA system), which is composed of toxin gene and its cognate antitoxin gene. It exists in bacteria, archaea, and fungi and toxins of it will lead to cell growth arrest or even cell death, while antitoxin will bond to corresponding toxin to form stable toxin-antitoxin complex and thus neutralize the toxicity of toxin. There are at least 33 TA systems in E. coli. Take ccdB and MazF, two of the toxin genes used in our project, for example. Under normal conditions, ccdB and MazF will bind with antitoxin ccdA and MazE respectively so the growth of E. coli won’t be affected. However, once E. coli faces severe conditions, ccdA and MazE will lose their function as they are degraded by protease. Therefore, the growth of E. coli will be suppressed. Researches shows that TA system plays an important role in organism as it may help the maintenance of plasmid and also allow organism to survive under stress.

   Base on the regulation of toxicity, TA system is divided into three types. In type I TA system, antitoxin gene mRNA will bind to toxin gene mRNA, causing toxin gene mRNA to degrade without being translated. That is, toxicity is regulated by toxin and antitoxin gene mRNA. Most type I TA system inhibit the synthesis of ATP. In type II TA system, toxicity is regulated by toxin and antitoxin protein by forming complex. Notice that the stability of antitoxin protein is lower in comparison to that of toxin protein. In order to continuously neutralization toxicity of toxic protein, antitoxin protein will be produced continuously. In our project, ccdB, MazF, chpBK, hipA, and yafQ are all members of type II TA system toxin protein but each of them works differently, details will be mentioned later. In type III TA system, antitoxin mRNA reacts directly with toxin protein to regular toxicity.
